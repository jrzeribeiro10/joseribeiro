About

I'm José Ribeiro, a young software developer with intensive experience in Unity and Unreal built over the past 3 years. I've been working on projects dealing with VR, AR, and 3D environments, always trying to put my skills into practice and be up to date with the evolution of the industry. I graduated from the University of Porto with a degree in Videogames and Multimedia Applications, which gave me good core bases in both technical and creative game development.

Apart from my academic background, I have gained very useful professional experience by working on game development projects throughout my summer holidays. I have always been in love with the creation of immersive worlds and interactive experiences that keep moving me forward to study new technologies and methodologies available in the field.

I am a developer who loves collaborative environments where my experience would be useful and, at the same time, learning from others. Always up for a challenge, pushing the limits of how much can be achieved in game development is something that interests me.

Portfolio

AR Romanesque Route

I created this augmented reality app based on 3 monuments on the Romanesque Route (Portugal). The models were designed using photogrammetry and the app created using Unity.

AR Inside Navigation

I created this app in Unity that indicates the path inside a house to a defined point. App calculates the fastest route by providing a plan.

Romanesque Route - Virtual Experience

I created this audio-visual experience about the Romanesque route (Rota do Românico-Portugal). Models created through photogrammetry. Made in Unreal Engine 5.

A.S.S (Airplane Saviour Simulator)

The project started in mid-September 2020 and lasted 6 months. I was directly linked to the Game and Level Design department in addition to programming mini games and game mechanics. It's an asymmetrical cooperative game in which two players work as a team to prevent a plane from crashing. While one person gives information to solve the puzzles, the other follows the instructions provided by his colleague.

SoW (Steel On Wheels)

Steel On Wheels was the first big 3D project I worked on. It is a racing and combat game. During this project I was directly linked to Programming (construction of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and UI), Game and Level Design.

Kiro

Kiro was a project that I initially conceived and worked on in the 1st semester of the 2nd year for 3 months as Game Design I. I was directly linked to Game Design, Level Design and artistic production. It's a 2D platform/puzzle game in which we control a character who uses a weapon with different functions.

PopCorn 2D Game

PopCorn 2D Game was a game that I designed and built for the 1st Year Programming Languages ​​course. This project was designed in 1 week and is a small 2D platform/Shooter game in which we control a character capable of moving and shooting at that we have to pick up objects to increase the score.
